Mathematics, DSP, Audio

Mathematics is another huge interest of mine, even though I am not very good at it. I have interest and experience in:

  • Algebra Fundamentals
  • Basic Statistics and Probability
  • Calculus Fundamentals / 3D Calculus / Differential Equation Applications
  • Mathematical Proofs / Set Theory / Basic Analysis
  • Basic Linear Algebra
  • Digital Signal Processing and Wave Analysis
  • Algorithm Analysis and Time Complexity

My biggest interest in the field of mathematics is Digital Signal Processing(DSP): the study of waves. DSP allows us to understand and analyze a sequence of numbers that represent samples of a continuous variable in a given domain(time, frequency, space, ect.). This has many applications in audio, video encoding, telecommunications, compression, radar, speech processing, to name a few. The topic of Fourier Analysis(FA), simplifying/approximating a function into simple trigonometric functions, is what interests me the most. In the context of DSP, FA allows us to break down complex waveforms into their base components, allowing us to understand them better. My understanding of this topic is still rudimentary, but I am constantly looking to improve my knowledge of DSP and waves.

I also fancy myself an Audio Engineer, as I find sound and the way computers store and work with it to be very interesting. I also consider Audio Synthesis, the process of creating sound from scratch (usually in the context of musical performance), to be one of my greatest interests. The mathematics and computer science behind generating sounds to be used in musical performance are very complex and interesting to me. This also includes topics grounded in music theory, such as how computers understand notes and how they sequence said notes to match with a given rhythm. With that being said, I am musically incompetent, meaning that I do not indulge in performance or musical composition(even though I sometimes wish I had the skills to :( ). Instead, I focus primarily on creating computer music technologies as a hobby, as well as manipulating and working with computer audio. Someday I would also like to get into music randomization, which is the process of creating pleasing music from random data.

TL;DR: Waves are sick, synths are cool, and audio programming is neato!
